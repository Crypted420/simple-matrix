Determinant of 2 by 2 Matrix

Det = (A . D) - (B . C)
The equation is used to find the determinant of a 2-by-2 matrix. To find the determinant, you first multiply the element from the top left to the bottom right corner and subtract the product from the top right to the bottom left. The determinant is most often used to find the nature of Solution off the system of linear equations defined by the matrix. This equation is only used for a 2 by 2 matrix, it cannot be used for matrices that are 3 by 3, 4 by 4 etc.
